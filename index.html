Aspergers

A neurological disorder that belongs to the autism spectrum disorders group (ASDs). Compared to the others, aspergers is classified as being on the mild end of the spectrum.

Symptoms

Healthline
  • Inept at social interactions
  • Having recurrent actions
  • Standing heavily by their thoughts
  • Primarily follow rules or routines

How does it develop?

Gale Library

Genes and one's surroundings mostly control whether one gets aspergers. There are studies showing that different folds in your brain also contribute to developing aspergers. There is a hypothesis that babies that have faced oxygen deprivation before birth later will develop it, as 50% of people with aspergers have experienced this. Young males are more likely to develop aspergers than females

Impacts

Succeed Socially

Misunderstandings may arise between this person and others because of their problems with socializing.

Treatment

Healthline

Cognitive behavioral therapy will help them to deal with the mental stress of their condition.

Bipolar Disorder 1

Associated with phases of drastic mood shifts ranging from rage to depression.

Symptoms

Web MD
  • Mania- Great energy and hyperactivity
  • Depression- rapid loss of interest in everything
  • Weight loss
  • Suicidal thoughts

How does it develop?

Health Line

A chemical imbalance in the brain. It can run in the family. Also, great stress or trauma can lead to developing bipolar disorder.

Impacts

Pacific Groove Hospital

Can lead to drug abuse to cope, harm their relationships and lead to them hurting themselves.

Treatment

Mayo Clinic

Medications are best to control one's emotions and prevent from going back to intense mania or depressive periods.

Bipolar Disorder 2

Associated with more moderate shifts in mood and slight hypomania which interchanges with periods of intense depression.

Symptoms

Bipolar Lives
  • Depression lasts for 2 weeks
  • Hypomania less drastic than bipolar 1
  • Riskier behavior
  • Lack of sleep

How does it develop?

Health Line

Bipolar 2 is more often diagnosed rather than bipolar 1 in the family. It is more common in females.

Impacts

Web MD

Most relatives of first degree relatives with bipolar 2 have bipolar 2 as well.

Depression

A variety of different state of minds that negatively impact the mood and mental state. The symptoms consist of a distressed mood, Less enthusiasm and lack of interest.

Symptoms

Web MD
  • Lack of interest with everyday activities
  • Suicidal thoughts
  • Feeling hopeless

How does it develop?

Verywell Mind

Different neurotransmitters in your brain sway your mood, like dopamine and serotonin. They play a role in depression, as do outside factors.

Impacts

Gale Library

Pessimissism and a lower mood included with feelings of wanting to suicide can be overwhelming. In 2016, 16.2 million US adults experienced a great depressive period in their lives. As for younger people, 6.4% of males and 19.4% faced a large depressive period in 2016. As more people confront depression, this afflicts their personal life and can reach out to others they know.

Treatment

Web MD

Create a solid routine to stick to. Reduce screentime at night if it is a distraction, to sleep better.

Eating Disorders

This condition is classified by having issues eating, and harboring harmful eating tendencies. It also consists of sub-divisions, like anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder. Marked by the symptoms of self-consciousness and weight issues

Symptoms

Verywell Mind
  • Anorexia- avoids eating
  • Bulimia- overconsumption and purging the food after
  • Binge Eating- overconsumption in great quantities of food

How does it develop?

Addiction Helper

Pressure to look a certain way or weight shaming may play a role in developing an eating disorder.

Impacts

Eating Disorder Hope

They can become separate from society, suffering alone and withstanding the stigma that may be against them by others and themselves.

Treatment

Mayo Clinic

Psychological therapy, helping them on their food choices, and seldom times medication.

Substance Abuse

Persistent addiction to any substance and matter which is deemed harmful to the health.

Symptoms

Web MD Drug Abuse
  • Loss of interest in hobbies
  • Stop taking care of your health
  • Spends time mostly by themselves
  • More irritatable
  • Bloodshot eyes and smaller pupils

How does it develop?

Lumen Learning

Can be passed down. Can be started if one has faced external or internal pain, and goes to a drug or substance to cope with it. Their status also can influence this development, if introduced to drugs for economic reasons.

Impacts

Serenity At Summit

Can cause a person's social circle to rip apart, as people can abandon them for becoming addicted. Having little communication with others, the person can develop social anxiety and depression and eventually overdose.

Treatment

Lumen Learning Verywell Mind

Therapy to treat these tendencies to use the substance, cue exposure therapy, counseling, medication.
